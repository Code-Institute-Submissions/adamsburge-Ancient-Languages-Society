About Us

In 1998, John Faroe, Nigel Horrocks and Jennifer Wasche started an ancient languages reading and conversation group to help them better understand the languages they were studying in university. The reading group aided their understanding of the languages so much that they decided to share their method with others. And thus the Ancient Languages Society was born.

Now, spread over three cities in the UK and Ireland, we offer instruction in several ancient languages, in small class sizes of no more than 8 students. We also offer weekly in-person meet-ups where individuals can practise various languages through active conversation and group readings of relevant texts.

All of our teachers are experts and have obtained PhD’s in their respective fields. As the aim of ALS is to preserve the knowledge of ancient languages, our classes are free of charge to any student who wishes to study with us. This is made possible by the generous support of the Dorothy Rainer foundation.

Teachers

John Faroe

John is currently Professor of Roman Literature at Edinburgh University. He read Classics and Ancient History at Balliol College, Oxford for both his undergraduate and graduate degrees.

Nigel Horrocks

Nigel is currently Professor of Greek Literature at Trinity College, Dublin. He read Classics and Ancient History at Queen’s College, Oxford for his undergraduate and graduate degrees.

Jennifer Wasche

Jennifer is currently Professor of Greek and Roman History at King’s College London. She read Classics and Ancient History at Brasenose College, Oxford from undergrad through to her DPhil.

Kyle Styles

Kyle is currently Lecturer of Egyptology at Trinity College, Dublin. He recently completed his PhD in Egyptology at the University of Edinburgh.

Stephanie Regle

Stephanie is an Associate Professor of Biblical Studies at Trinity College, Dublin. She completed her DPhil in Theology and Religious studies at Cambridge.

Lindsay Benin

Lindsay is currently Professor of Assyriology at Edinburgh University. She read for her DPhil in Oriental Studies at Wolfson College, Oxford.
